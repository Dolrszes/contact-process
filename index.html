Introduction

The industrial process chosen for this project is the production of sulfuric acid using the Contact Process. Sulfuric acid was first produced on an industrial scale in the eighteenth century, while the modern Contact Process was developed in the late nineteenth century to improve efficiency and safety.

Sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) is one of the most important industrial chemicals worldwide. It is widely used in the production of fertilizers, detergents, dyes, explosives, and petroleum refining. A large proportion of sulfuric acid is used to manufacture phosphate fertilizers, which are essential for modern agriculture and global food security.

The Contact Process demonstrates how scientific knowledge is applied to solve real-world industrial problems. By applying principles of chemical kinetics, equilibrium, and catalysis, sulfur dioxide can be efficiently converted into sulfuric acid on a large scale.
