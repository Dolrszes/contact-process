Impacts of the Process

Evaluation · Economic factor · Environmental factor

Economic factor

2 advantages · 1 disadvantage

Advantages

A major economic advantage of the Contact Process is its high efficiency. The use of a vanadium(V) oxide (V₂O₅) catalyst allows sulfur dioxide to be converted to sulfur trioxide at a fast rate, reducing energy consumption and lowering operational costs.

Sulfuric acid produced by the Contact Process is essential for industries such as fertiliser manufacturing, petroleum refining, and chemical production. This supports agricultural productivity and contributes to economic growth and food security on both national and global levels.

Disadvantage

A significant economic disadvantage is the high initial cost of setting up Contact Process plants. Advanced equipment, catalysts, and safety systems require large capital investment and continuous maintenance, which can be financially challenging, especially for developing economies.

Environmental factor

2 advantages · 1 disadvantage

Advantages

When properly controlled, the Contact Process significantly reduces sulfur dioxide emissions by converting SO₂ into sulfuric acid instead of releasing it into the atmosphere. This helps to reduce air pollution and the formation of acid rain.

Modern Contact Process plants recover heat from exothermic reaction stages to improve energy efficiency. This reduces fuel consumption and lowers the overall environmental footprint of sulfuric acid production.

Disadvantage

If equipment failure occurs, leaks of SO₂ or SO₃ may cause air pollution and acidification. In addition, sulfuric acid spills can result in long-term environmental damage if not quickly contained and properly managed.
