Impacts of the Process

Evaluation · Economic factor · Environmental factor

Economic factor

2 advantages + 1 disadvantage

Advantages

One major economic advantage of the Contact Process is its high efficiency and ability to operate continuously on a large industrial scale. This reduces the cost per unit of sulfuric acid and ensures a stable supply for industries such as fertilizer production and petroleum refining.

Another advantage is the relatively low cost of raw materials. Sulfur dioxide is often obtained as a by-product from petroleum refining and metal extraction, reducing waste and lowering production costs.

Disadvantage

A key disadvantage is the high initial capital investment required. Corrosion-resistant materials and specialised equipment (converter, heat exchangers, absorption towers) increase construction and long-term maintenance costs.

Environmental factor

2 advantages + 1 disadvantage

Advantages

A major environmental advantage is the reduction of sulfur dioxide emissions when the process is properly controlled. SO₂, a contributor to acid rain, is converted into sulfuric acid instead of being released into the atmosphere.

Modern plants also recover heat from exothermic stages to improve energy efficiency, which reduces the overall environmental footprint of sulfuric acid production.

Disadvantage

The process can cause environmental harm if failures occur. Leaks of SO₂ or SO₃ may lead to air pollution and acidification, while sulfuric acid spills can result in long-term damage to ecosystems if not contained.
