Conclusion

The Contact Process is expected to remain the primary method for industrial sulfuric acid production due to its efficiency, reliability, and economic importance. Sulfuric acid will continue to be essential for fertilizer production and other key industries.

Ongoing research focuses on improving catalyst performance and reducing energy consumption by operating at lower temperatures. Stricter environmental regulations are also driving the development of safer technologies and improved gas purification systems.

In the future, increased use of sulfur dioxide recovered from industrial waste streams may further reduce emissions and support more sustainable industrial practices. Overall, the Contact Process will continue to evolve as scientific advancements are applied to minimise environmental impact while maintaining economic value.
