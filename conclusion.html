Conclusion

Evaluation · Future development · Sustainability

The Contact Process remains the most effective method for the industrial production of sulfuric acid due to its high efficiency, use of catalysis, and ability to operate continuously on a large scale. Sulfuric acid is essential for fertiliser production, chemical manufacturing, and petroleum refining, making the process economically important worldwide.

Although the Contact Process offers economic and environmental advantages, it still requires careful control to minimise emissions and environmental risks. High conversion efficiency, catalyst reuse, and heat recovery systems reduce waste, but failures can result in pollution if not properly managed.

Future developments are likely to focus on improving catalyst efficiency, reducing energy consumption, and increasing sustainability. Overall, the Contact Process demonstrates how scientific principles are applied to balance economic benefits with environmental responsibility in modern industry.

Key idea: efficiency must be balanced with environmental control
