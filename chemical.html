Chemical Process

Production of sulfur dioxide

Sulfur dioxide is produced by burning sulfur in air or by roasting metal sulfide ores. The reaction occurs at high temperatures and provides sulfur dioxide as the main reactant for the Contact Process.

S (s) + O₂ (g) → SO₂ (g)

Oxidation of sulfur dioxide to sulfur trioxide

Sulfur dioxide is oxidized to sulfur trioxide in a reversible reaction carried out at approximately 450 °C and atmospheric pressure. A vanadium(V) oxide (V₂O₅) catalyst is used to increase the reaction rate.

2 SO₂ (g) + O₂ (g) ⇌ 2 SO₃ (g)

Absorption and formation of sulfuric acid

Sulfur trioxide is absorbed in concentrated sulfuric acid to form oleum, which is then diluted with water to produce sulfuric acid.

SO₃ (g) + H₂SO₄ (l) → H₂S₂O₇ (l)
H₂S₂O₇ (l) + H₂O (l) → 2 H₂SO₄ (l)
