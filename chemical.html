Chemical Process

Reaction sequence · Conditions · Catalyst V₂O₅ · Oleum formation

Production of sulfur dioxide

Sulfur dioxide is produced by burning sulfur in air or by roasting metal sulfide ores. The reaction occurs at high temperatures and provides sulfur dioxide as the main reactant for the Contact Process.

S (s) + O₂ (g) → SO₂ (g)

Oxidation of sulfur dioxide to sulfur trioxide

Sulfur dioxide is oxidised to sulfur trioxide in a reversible reaction carried out at approximately 450 °C and near atmospheric pressure. A vanadium(V) oxide (V₂O₅) catalyst increases the reaction rate. The operating temperature is a compromise between reaction rate and equilibrium yield.

2 SO₂ (g) + O₂ (g) ⇌ 2 SO₃ (g)

Absorption and formation of sulfuric acid

Sulfur trioxide is not absorbed directly into water because the reaction is highly exothermic and produces acid mist. Instead, SO₃ is absorbed in concentrated sulfuric acid to form oleum, which is then diluted to produce sulfuric acid.

SO₃ (g) + H₂SO₄ (l) → H₂S₂O₇ (l)
H₂S₂O₇ (l) + H₂O (l) → 2 H₂SO₄ (l)
